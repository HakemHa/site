HTML Documentation

Tag Description
DOCTYPE html Specifies the MarkUp language
html All content of your webpage must go inside (html)(/html) tags.
head Contains information about the webpage. The title tag goes inside (head)(/head) tags.
meta
title Title of the webpage (what appears in the window/tab of your browser). The text itself does not appear on webpage.
body Everything that appears on the webpage should go between these tags
p Defines a paragraph (text with some space on the bottom and top).
h(1-6) Gives the content a special size depending on number that is used to divide sections according to importance.
lang Defines language of text
Notes on Jon Duckett HTML/CSS book

Structure

Structures help pages be easier to understand, headings and sub-headings can be used to divide different sections of the page. This is the whole point of HTML, meaning HyperText MarkUp Language it describes the structure of pages, it "describes" the structure using elements made of tags, most having an opening and a closing tag. Tags can contain attributes that give more specificity to their function, they are written as name= "value" , those are always on the opening tag after the character (and are white on table). The structure always follow DOCTYPE->html->head->title html->body.
